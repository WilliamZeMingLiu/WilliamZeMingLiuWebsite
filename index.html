About Me

Born and raised in Hong Kong, I spent my whole K-12 education attending Hong Kong International School. I was accepted into USC in 2017 as an Economics major, however early in my freshman year my interest in programming web applications grew. I was also interested in business management, specifically the business behind growing startup companies. Thankfully, USC offered a double major program that allowed me to study both a Computer Science degree and Business Administration degree, exposing me to both the business and engineering aspects of technology. I am currently seeking employment as a software engineer for 2021.

My most recent internship was interning at JUMP Investors, a venture capital company that is run by the co-founder of Akamai Technologies, Randall Kaplan. I decided to work at a VC because I wanted to explore the business of technology companies as well. I learned a great deal from reviewing pitch decks, drafting reports, and data entry for JUMP's in-house startup company, Sandee. In fact, I was the intern that provided the most amount of content creation for Sandee's website during that summer.

My previous internship was an quality assurance intern position at Aura Labs, a small startup company based in Hong Kong that builds websites and mobile applications for companies. This was my first experience working in an office, and the fact that this company comprised of less than 10 people at the time made me interested in working for startups in the future. Through my role, I was able to help identify over 20 bugs/mistakes on a client's website, ensuring that the application was published with zero bugs.

My first job was a teacher assistant position for Hong Kong International School Summer School Program. I enjoy teaching because it is amazing to see young students being enthusiastic about learning new things. I enjoyed my time as a TA so greatly that I decided to do it again the following summer. This experience with teaching will not be the last and I hope to combine my passion for programming and teaching in the future.
